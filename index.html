SkyWatch RF — Counter-Drone Detection Board

Hackathon 2026

A $61 RF detection board that passively detects drone control signals at 2.4 GHz and 5.8 GHz using an all-analog signal chain: an LNA, RF switch, dual bandpass filters, and a log detector. WiFi false-alarms are rejected through bandwidth-based power density discrimination and an RC pulse-width filter — validated in LTSpice before hardware build. Alerts are relayed wirelessly via a LoRa radio at 915 MHz, completely outside the monitored bands, with a theoretical detection range of ~950 m. PCB laid out on Rogers 4003C for stable RF performance at 5.8 GHz.

DC Motor RPM Controller

Dec 2025

A fully analog/digital closed-loop DC motor speed controller built from discrete ICs for ENPH 259 at UBC. A phototransistor encoder feeds an 8-bit counter clocked by a Schmitt-trigger oscillator; a D-latch samples the count at 5 Hz and holds it steady while the counter resets. An R-2R ladder DAC converts the digital count to a voltage, which an op-amp PI controller compares against the setpoint to drive an NPN BJT motor stage. Achieves 0–3480 RPM across a 0–2.5 V setpoint range, with a maximum angular speed of 364 rad/s.

BMS Current Sensor Calibration — UBC Formula Electric

2026

Wrote the calibration routine for the BMS tractive-system current sensors — a 400 A and a 50 A channel. With a known current run through the sensor, the script reads the board’s ADC live over Chimera — UBC Formula Electric’s tool for driving a board’s peripherals (ADC, I²C, GPIO) straight from a Python script over USB — collects the calibration points, and fits a linear map between ADC voltage and current with NumPy. It exports the fit coefficients in both directions so the firmware can turn raw ADC readings into accurate live current.
